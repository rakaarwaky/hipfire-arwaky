# benchmarks/results — Retention Policy

## What belongs here (commit freely)

| Type | Examples | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Structured summaries | `*.md`, `*.json` | Human- and machine-readable; small |
| Aggregate perf tables | `2026-04-26-perf-regression-recovery.json` | Distilled, not raw |
| Devlog narratives | `devlog_*.md` | Context for future sessions |

## What does NOT belong here (never commit)

| Type | Pattern | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Raw stderr/stdout logs | `**/*.log` | Large, noisy, non-reproducible context |
| Raw f32 tensor dumps | `**/*.f32` | Multi-MB per file; regenerable via replay |
| `.npy` activation captures | Moved to `benchmarks/references/*_activations/` | Regenerated by `scripts/capture_dots_ocr_activations.py` |

Both `*.log` and `*.f32` patterns are gitignored in the repo root `.gitignore`.

## Retention window for `.md`/`.json` summaries

- **Keep indefinitely:** any result cited in a PR, MEMORY.md entry, or published comparison
- **Prune at 90 days:** ad-hoc devlogs that were never cited and have no `TODO` / decision context

Pruning is manual (no automation); use `git log --diff-filter=A -- benchmarks/results/` to find
old additions and decide per-file.

## Relationship to quality-baselines

`benchmarks/quality-baselines/results/*.kldseq` files are research anchors — they are
intentionally tracked regardless of size. They represent calibrated, md5-anchored eval
sequences that gate cross-commit quality claims. Do not apply the log/f32 policy to them.

See `benchmarks/quality-baselines/` for the full policy on those files.
